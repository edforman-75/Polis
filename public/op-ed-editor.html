Op-Ed Structure

1. Hook & Context

Start with a compelling anecdote, statistic, or question that draws readers in and establishes the issue's importance.

2. Thesis Statement

Clearly state your main argument or position. What specific action or change are you advocating for?

3. Supporting Evidence

Present 2-3 key arguments with facts, data, expert opinions, or personal experience to support your thesis.

4. Address Opposition

Acknowledge counterarguments and explain why your position is stronger or more compelling.

5. Call to Action

End with specific steps readers can take or a clear vision of what success looks like.

Quick Quality Checklist
  • Clear, arguable thesis statement
  • Compelling opening hook
  • 3+ pieces of supporting evidence
  • At least one counterargument addressed
  • Specific call to action
  • Personal credibility/stake established
  • Proper word count (600-800 words)

Op-Ed Writing Tips

What Makes a Great Op-Ed?

  • Timely: Responds to current events or issues
  • Personal: Includes your unique perspective or experience
  • Specific: Proposes concrete solutions or actions
  • Accessible: Written for a general audience
  • Persuasive: Uses evidence and emotion effectively

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Starting with background everyone knows
  • Making too many different arguments
  • Using overly academic or technical language
  • Failing to address obvious counterarguments
  • Ending without a clear call to action

Submission Tips

  • Research the publication's op-ed guidelines
  • Include a brief bio highlighting relevant credentials
  • Suggest a headline (editors may change it)
  • Be prepared to make revisions quickly
  • Follow up professionally if you don't hear back

📋 Professional Op-Ed Creation Workflow

Total Timeline: 4-6 days for comprehensive op-ed creation and submission

📋 Phase 1: Planning & Research (1-2 days)

Step 1: Topic Selection & Angle
  • Identify your unique perspective and expertise
  • Choose timely relevance to current events
  • Define one clear, defensible position
  • Check for topic oversaturation in recent coverage
Step 2: Audience & Publication Research
  • Read recent op-eds from target outlets
  • Understand each publication's editorial stance
  • Note word count requirements (usually 600-800 words)
  • Review specific submission guidelines
Step 3: Evidence Gathering
  • Collect supporting statistics, studies, expert quotes
  • Document all sources for citations
  • Identify likely counterarguments and prepare responses
  • Gather relevant personal anecdotes and examples

✍️ Phase 2: Writing (1-2 days)

Step 4: Structure Development
  • Opening Hook: Compelling anecdote, statistic, or question
  • Thesis Statement: Clear, specific argument with preview
  • Body (3-4 paragraphs): One main point per paragraph, strongest first
  • Call to Action: Specific steps and broader implications
Step 5: Draft Creation
  • Focus on getting ideas down, don't self-edit initially
  • Stay within target word count range
  • Keep paragraphs short for readability
  • Use active voice and write with conviction

🔍 Phase 3: Revision & Polish (1-2 days)

Step 6: Content Review
  • Ensure each paragraph advances your thesis
  • Verify all sources are credible and current
  • Check logical flow between ideas
  • Confirm counterarguments are addressed
Step 7: Style & Voice
  • Strike conversational authority balance
  • Eliminate jargon, define technical terms
  • Mix sentence lengths for rhythm
  • Include relevant personal connection
Step 8: Technical Polish
  • Fact-check all statistics and claims
  • Format citations per publication style
  • Read aloud to catch awkward phrasing
  • Trim or expand to meet word requirements

📤 Phase 4: Submission & Follow-up (Ongoing)

Step 9: Submission Strategy
  • Submit to most preferred publication first
  • Only submit to one outlet at a time
  • Write brief, compelling pitch email (2-3 sentences)
  • Prepare 1-2 sentence author bio with credentials
Step 10: Timeline Management
  • Most publications respond within 1-2 weeks
  • One polite follow-up after stated timeframe
  • Have 2-3 alternative publications ready
  • Be prepared to move quickly for time-sensitive topics

⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Preaching to the choir instead of persuading
  • Using academic tone for general readers
  • Weak conclusions that only summarize
  • Burying your strongest point
  • Reactive criticism without solutions
